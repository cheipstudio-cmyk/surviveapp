Privacy Policy

Overview

SURVIVE. is an emergency preparedness calculator developed by Cheip Studio (Eugenio Casale). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data the app collects and how it is handled.

Data We Collect

We do not collect any personal data. SURVIVE. is designed to work entirely offline. The app does not require an internet connection, does not have user accounts, and does not transmit any information to external servers.

Local Storage

The app stores your preferences and checklist progress locally on your device using AsyncStorage. This data includes: your selected language, number of days and people, checked items, and open category states. This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or any third party.

GPS / Location

The "My Coordinates" feature uses your device's GPS to display your current position. GPS coordinates are processed entirely on your device. They are never transmitted, stored remotely, or shared with anyone. The GPS feature only activates when you explicitly open the coordinates screen.

Third-Party Services

SURVIVE. does not use analytics, advertising, tracking, or any third-party services. No cookies are set. No data is shared with third parties.

Children's Privacy

The app does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. Since no personal data is collected from any user, the app is safe for use by people of all ages.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.

Description of Service

SURVIVE. is an emergency preparedness calculator that helps users estimate the supplies needed for emergency scenarios. It includes a survival guide with practical tips for various emergency situations.

The survival guide contains general advice compiled from publicly available sources. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, first aid training, or official emergency instructions from local authorities.

Always follow instructions from local emergency services and authorities. In a medical emergency, call your local emergency number (112 in Europe, 911 in the US).
